How do I know if I qualify for the VitaCare Patient Assistance Program?
+
Eligibility for our Patient Assistance Program is based on household income, insurance status, and the specific medicine prescribed. Generally, patients who are uninsured or underinsured and meet income thresholds (typically <400% of the federal poverty level) may qualify. You can check your eligibility online in 2 minutes or call our helpline at 1800-VITA-PAP. Your doctor can also submit the application on your behalf using our Physician Authorization Form.
What should I do if I miss a dose of my VitaCare medicine?
+
The answer depends on the specific medicine and how close your next scheduled dose is. As a general rule: if you remember within a few hours, take the missed dose. If it's close to the next dose time, skip the missed dose and continue your regular schedule. Never take a double dose to make up for a missed one. For specific guidance, refer to the patient information leaflet in your medicine pack or call our 24/7 MediHelpline at 1800-VITA-007.
Can I take my VitaCare medicine during pregnancy or while breastfeeding?
+
This is an important question that must be answered by your doctor for your specific medicine and situation. Many medicines are contraindicated during pregnancy or lactation while others are considered safe. Do not stop or start any medicine during pregnancy without consulting your healthcare provider. Our Medical Affairs team can provide detailed prescribing information to your doctor upon request.
How should I store my VitaCare medicines properly?
+
Storage requirements vary by medicine type. Most tablets and capsules should be stored at room temperature (15–30°C), away from direct sunlight and moisture. Insulin pens and biologics typically require refrigeration (2–8°C). Inhalers should not be frozen or left in hot cars. Check the specific storage instructions on your medicine label or download the patient information leaflet. Our downloadable storage guide covers all VitaCare products.
What are the most common side effects of VitaCare medicines and when should I be concerned?
+
Side effects vary by medicine class. Common, manageable effects include nausea (especially in first weeks), mild headache, or slight dizziness. Seek immediate medical attention for: difficulty breathing, severe allergic reaction (hives, swelling of face/throat), chest pain, or sudden vision/speech changes. Report all adverse events to our Pharmacovigilance team at 1800-VITA-ADR. All serious adverse events are documented and reported to regulatory authorities.
Can I travel internationally with my VitaCare prescription medicines?
+
Yes, in most countries you can travel with prescribed medicines. Best practices: carry medicines in original packaging with your prescription, get a letter from your doctor on letterhead, check the destination country's regulations (some medicines like certain opioids require advance permits), keep temperature-sensitive medicines in an insulated bag with ice packs. Contact our helpline or email medicalinfo@vitacare.com for a travel letter template.
My doctor wants to switch me from a branded VitaCare medicine to a generic. Is this safe?
+
Generic medicines contain the same active ingredient at the same dose and are required to demonstrate bioequivalence to the branded medicine. Switching is generally safe and decided by your doctor based on your health profile. However, for certain medicines like narrow-therapeutic-index drugs (e.g., anticoagulants, thyroid hormones, antiepileptics), your doctor may prefer to keep you on the same formulation for stability. Always discuss any switch with your prescribing doctor.
How do I report a problem or adverse reaction to a VitaCare medicine?
+
Report adverse events through any of these channels: Call our 24/7 Pharmacovigilance Helpline at 1800-VITA-ADR, email pv@vitacare.com, submit online via our Adverse Event Reporting Form, or ask your doctor/pharmacist to report on your behalf. We take all reports seriously and investigate every case. Your report contributes to medicine safety monitoring and patient protection globally.

Medication Safety — Know Before You Take

Essential safety information every patient should know about their medicines.

⚠️

Drug Interactions

HIGH PRIORITY

Some medicines interact with each other, with food, or with herbal supplements — potentially reducing effectiveness or causing harm.

  • Always show your doctor a complete list of ALL medicines, vitamins, and supplements you take
  • Avoid grapefruit juice with statins, calcium channel blockers, and certain immunosuppressants
  • St. John's Wort can reduce effectiveness of blood thinners, HIV medicines, and contraceptives
  • Alcohol can interact dangerously with blood thinners, sedatives, and metformin
📦

Storage Instructions

IMPORTANT

Improper storage can reduce medicine potency or cause degradation — always follow the storage instructions on your label.

  • Store most tablets at room temperature (15–30°C) away from moisture and light
  • Refrigerate insulin (2–8°C) — never freeze; discard if frozen
  • Never store medicines in the bathroom — humidity degrades them
  • Keep all medicines out of reach of children and pets
🔄

Missed Dose Protocol

ADHERENCE

Missing doses reduces medicine effectiveness and can cause rebound effects for some medicines like blood pressure drugs.

  • If you remember within a few hours, take the dose immediately
  • If it's close to your next dose, skip the missed one — never double dose
  • For once-weekly medicines (e.g., osteoporosis), take the missed dose as soon as you remember if the next dose is >3 days away
  • Use our MyCare app for automated dose reminders
🚨

Side Effect Recognition

EMERGENCY

Know the difference between common manageable side effects and warning signs that need immediate medical attention.

  • Seek emergency care for: difficulty breathing, severe allergic reaction (hives, facial swelling), chest pain, sudden confusion
  • Call your doctor for: persistent nausea/vomiting, unusual bleeding, jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes), vision changes
  • Common effects (mild, resolve within 2 weeks): nausea, headache, mild dizziness
  • Report all adverse events to 1800-VITA-ADR
